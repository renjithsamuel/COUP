Coup is a 2–6 player bluffing game where you aim to be the last player with hidden influence (cards) remaining. Each player starts with two face-down cards and two coins. On your turn, take one action to gain money, steal, or eliminate others, often lying about which characters you hold to win. 
Objective
Be the last player with at least one hidden card. If both your cards are revealed (face up), you are out.

Setup
Deal Cards: Give each player 2 cards face-down (keep them secret).
Coins: Give each player 2 coins.
Treasury: Place remaining cards and coins in the middle.

Turn Actions
On your turn, take one action: 
YouTube
YouTube
Income: Take 1 coin from the treasury (cannot be blocked).
Foreign Aid: Take 2 coins from the treasury (can be blocked by Duke).
Coup: Pay 7 coins to force another player to lose 1 influence (cannot be blocked).
Character Actions: Claim to have a character to use their special ability (even if you don't actually have the card).
Duke (Tax): Take 3 coins from the treasury.
Assassin (Assassinate): Pay 3 coins to force a player to lose 1 influence.
Captain (Steal): Steal 2 coins from another player.
Ambassador (Exchange): Draw 2 cards, return 2 to the deck.

Challenges and Blocks
Challenge: If you claim a character action, any player can challenge you. You must show the card. If you cannot (or won't), you lose an influence. If you can, the challenger loses an influence.
Block: Certain actions can be blocked by specific characters (e.g., Contessa blocks Assassination, Captain/Ambassador blocks Stealing). A block can also be challenged.

Key Rules
If you start your turn with 10 or more coins, you must perform a Coup.
When a card is revealed (lost influence), it stays face-up on the table.
If you lose a challenge, you lose an influence
